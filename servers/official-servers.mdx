The official GT New Horizons servers are run directly by the GTNH development team and are hosted primarily in Europe. These servers maintain the latest stable (and, on test instances, pre-release) versions of the pack and are moderated by the core team. All official servers are whitelisted — you must apply and be accepted before you can connect.

## Applying for access

Applications are open to everyone. There are two ways to apply:

- Submit an application at [gtnewhorizons.com](https://www.gtnewhorizons.com/)
- Use the `!apply` command in the `#server-applications` channel of the [GTNH Discord](https://discord.gg/gtnh)

Both methods will walk you through the same process. After submitting, wait for an approval notification before attempting to connect.

## Server rules and conduct

Official servers follow the same conduct standards as the GTNH Discord. Broadly, the expectations are:

- Respect other players and staff.
- Do not grief, steal from, or sabotage other players' builds.
- Do not exploit bugs or duplication glitches. Report them instead.
- Do not use client-side cheats or automation tools that give unfair advantages.
- Keep chat civil and on-topic.

The full rules are posted on the GTNH website and in the Discord. Repeated or serious violations can result in removal from the whitelist.

## Performance notes

GregTech modpacks generate significant server load, particularly during machine-heavy automation and chunk loading. The official servers are optimized and maintained by the team, but expect some TPS (ticks per second) variation during peak hours when many players are active simultaneously. If you notice lag, check the `#server-status` channel in Discord before filing a report — performance dips during high-traffic periods are normal.

<Tip>
  Check the GTNH Discord #server-status channel for current uptime and maintenance windows.
</Tip>
