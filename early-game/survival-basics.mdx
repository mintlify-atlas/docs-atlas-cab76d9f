---
title: "Surviving Your First Days in GTNH"
description: "GTNH's early game is brutal. Learn how hardcore darkness, food variety requirements, and dangerous mobs make first nights a genuine fight for survival."
---

GT New Horizons does not ease you in gently. From the moment you spawn, the world is actively working against you: nights are genuinely pitch black, food grows stale if you rely on the same meal, and the mobs roaming in the dark are far more dangerous than anything you faced in vanilla Minecraft. Every decision in the first few days matters — where you build, what you eat, and whether you have a light source ready before sunset.

## Hardcore darkness

When night falls in GTNH, it falls completely. Without a light source or an active potion effect, the world outside your shelter is functionally invisible. This is not just atmospheric — hostile mobs spawn freely in the dark, and you will not see them coming.

Getting a bed is the single most important first-night priority. If materials are too scarce to craft a full bed immediately, consider a sleeping bag as an alternative.

<Tip>
  Sleeping bags from the SleepingBags mod skip the night without setting your spawn point, making them useful early on when wool is hard to come by. Craft one with three wool in a row.
</Tip>

Once you are past the first night, invest in permanent lighting. GTNH provides several options beyond basic torches:

- **Torches** — the immediate default; cheap but consumed over time in some configurations
- **Lanterns** from Chisel — decorative and effective
- **Project Red lights** — compact and easy to place
- **Floodlights** — powerful area lighting for larger bases, requiring a small amount of power

Light up your entire base perimeter before you consider exploring further.

## Food and Spice of Life

GTNH includes **Spice of Life**, which tracks your food history and applies diminishing returns when you eat the same food repeatedly. The mod stores the last 20 foods you have eaten and reduces both hunger and saturation restored when a food appears too frequently. The formula kicks in after eating the same item four or more times within that window, and its effect compounds with how varied your overall diet has been.

In practice, this means you cannot survive indefinitely on a stack of bread or cooked chicken. You need variety.

Early food sources to diversify with:

- **Berries** from Natura — spawn in the wild, no farming required
- **Seeds** — grass drops various Pam's HarvestCraft seeds; eat the produce as you farm
- **Hunting** — raw or cooked meat from animals counts as different food items per species
- **Villages** — village gardens often contain HarvestCraft crops ready to harvest

<Note>
  Hunger Overhaul is also active and reduces the base hunger value of most foods. Food restores less than you are used to, and low hunger imposes slowness, weakness, and mining penalties. Keep your hunger bar topped up.
</Note>

Pam's HarvestCraft adds dozens of crops and prepared foods. As soon as you find a stable water source, start a small farm with whatever seeds you have. Even four or five different crops will keep Spice of Life satisfied in the early game.

## Dangerous mobs

Two mods change what you encounter at night in ways that vanilla does not prepare you for.

**Infernal Mobs** randomly selects some mobs and grants them one or more special abilities — fire immunity, healing, speed boosts, the ability to call reinforcements, or the ability to reflect projectiles. An infernal zombie with a glowing name is not just a stronger zombie; it is a different encounter requiring a different approach. Flee if you are not equipped to fight.

**Special Mobs** replaces standard mob variants with new, more challenging subtypes. Skeletons may have better accuracy or carry different weapons. Creepers may be charged or otherwise enhanced.

Do not fight at night unless you have no choice. Stay inside, tend your furnaces, and let the sun handle cleanup.

## Wood and stone tools

GregTech enforces a strict tool progression. You cannot craft iron tools directly from a crafting table the way vanilla Minecraft allows. The pack requires you to progress through the stone tier before iron tools become craftable.

<Warning>
  Do not rush to iron. GregTech progression requires going through the stone tool tier first. Attempting to skip ahead will leave you with materials you cannot yet use.
</Warning>

This also means your first tools — wooden axe, wooden pickaxe, stone tools — need to last longer than they would in a standard playthrough. Repair them when possible, and avoid using a higher-durability tool for tasks a lower one can handle.

## Your first shelter

A shelter in GTNH is not optional decoration. It is the difference between surviving and dying on your first night. Your shelter needs to accomplish three things:

1. Block mob spawns (solid roof and walls, no gaps)
2. Provide interior lighting (even a single torch is enough)
3. Give you a place to sleep to skip the night

Do not prioritize aesthetics or size early on. A small, well-lit box with a bed is better than a large unlit structure. Once you have a safe base, you can begin processing resources and planning your progression into the Bronze Age.

## First-night survival checklist

<Steps>
  <Step title="Gather wood">
    Punch trees immediately on spawn. Collect at least 16–20 logs. You need these for tools, a crafting table, and a bed frame.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Craft basic tools">
    Make a wooden pickaxe and axe first. Use the pickaxe to get stone, then upgrade to stone tools. A stone sword is essential before night.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find food">
    Look for berry bushes from Natura, kill animals for meat, or harvest any visible crops near a village. Eat multiple different food types to start building food variety.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Gather wool for a bed">
    Kill three sheep to get enough wool. Combine with three wooden planks to craft a bed. If wool is scarce, craft a sleeping bag from three wool instead.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Build a shelter">
    Before the sun sets, dig into a hillside or construct a small structure. Seal all openings, place a torch inside, and put down your bed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sleep through the first night">
    Use the bed as soon as the night begins. This skips the danger and resets to morning so you can explore safely.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Open the questbook (press **B** by default) and navigate to the **Introduction** tab. It contains guided first steps written specifically for new players and highlights the most important early objectives in the correct order.
</Note>
