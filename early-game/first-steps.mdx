title: "First Steps: From Wood to Steam Age"
description: "A guide to GTNH's early game progression arc, from punching trees on day one through building your first steam-powered GregTech machines."
---

GT New Horizons has a clearly defined early-game arc, and understanding it upfront saves a great deal of frustration. You begin with nothing, work through a stone and bronze tool phase, build your first GregTech multi-block machines, and eventually reach the Steam Age — where machines powered by boilers begin doing the heavy lifting. This is not a sprint; the pack is designed so that each stage has meaningful depth before the next one opens up. Rushing past a step typically means running into a wall you cannot bypass.

<Note>
  The questbook's **GT Progression** tab walks you through each of these milestones with detailed guidance, required items, and explanations of why each step matters. Open it with **B** and check it regularly.
</Note>

## Progression milestones

<Steps>
  <Step title="Punch trees and survive night 1">
    Gather wood, craft basic tools, find food, and build a shelter before dark. Kill animals for meat and look for berry bushes to diversify your diet early — Spice of Life penalizes eating the same food repeatedly. Make a bed or sleeping bag and sleep through the first night.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the Bronze Age">
    Bronze is your first major material milestone. Find copper and tin ore veins — GregTech spawns ore in large localized veins rather than scattered clusters, so explore a wide area if your starting location seems dry. Smelt the ores into ingots, then combine two tin and three copper to alloy into bronze. Bronze tools are significantly more durable than stone and unlock the next tier of recipes.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Craft your first GregTech tools">
    Vanilla tools get you started, but GregTech-specific tools are required for many machine assembly steps. Craft the following as early as possible:
    - **Hammer** — used for crafting metal plates and some components
    - **File** — shapes ingots into rods and other forms
    - **Screwdriver** — required for assembling machines
    - **Wrench** — used to rotate and dismantle machines

    Each of these is crafted with GregTech recipes. Check NEI (press **R** on any item) to see the exact recipe.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Build a Coke Oven">
    The Coke Oven is your first GregTech multi-block machine and a critical bottleneck for the rest of early-game progression. It is built from Coke Oven Bricks arranged in a 3×3×3 hollow cube. The Coke Oven converts coal into **Coal Coke**, which burns twice as long as regular coal, and produces **Creosote Oil** as a byproduct — needed for Treated Wood Planks and later for railroad infrastructure.

    <Warning>
      Do not skip the Coke Oven. It is a required step for producing steel, which blocks progression into the Steel Age and beyond. There is no alternative route.
    </Warning>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set up Bronze Steam Machines">
    With coal coke available, build a **Bronze Boiler** (Steam Boiler) and connect it to your first tier of steam-powered GregTech machines:
    - **Steam Macerator** — crushes ores into two dust, doubling ore output when smelted
    - **Steam Extractor** — extracts rubber and other materials from blocks
    - **Steam Compressor** — compresses items into denser forms

    The boiler runs on solid fuel. Coal coke is the best early fuel source. Fill the boiler with water and fuel, and it will begin generating steam for your machines.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Process your first ores">
    Run your ores through the Steam Macerator before smelting them. One ore processed in the macerator yields two dust, and two dust smelted in a furnace yields two ingots. This effectively doubles your ore output from the very start.

    The processing chain is: **Ore → Macerator → 2x Dust → Furnace → 2x Ingots**

    This step alone makes a significant difference in how quickly you accumulate materials for machines and tools.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Produce iron and steel">
    Iron is smelted normally, but **steel** requires the **Blast Furnace** — another GregTech multi-block. The Blast Furnace is built from Heat-Proof Machine Casings and processes iron with coal coke (or other carbon sources) to produce steel ingots. Steel is stronger and more durable than iron, and it is required for the next tier of machines and many late-game components.

    Build the Blast Furnace as soon as you have the materials. Steel unlocks the transition out of the Steam Age.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Using NEI to look up recipes

<Tip>
  Press **R** while hovering over any item in your inventory or in NEI to see all crafting recipes for that item. Press **U** to see all recipes that use it as an ingredient. NEI is your most important tool for understanding the pack — consult it constantly, especially for GregTech machine and component recipes.
</Tip>

GregTech adds many non-standard crafting recipes that require specific tools (like the Hammer or File) to be held in your hand while crafting. NEI will show these requirements in the recipe display. If a recipe shows items in an unusual pattern with extra tool icons, that is why.

## A note on Thaumcraft

The Thaumominicon (Thaumcraft's research journal) becomes available in the early game when you craft a Thaumominicon item or encounter your first aura node. Thaumcraft research is deep and rewarding, but it is not urgent in the first stages of play.

Collect Thaumcraft research points and scan items as you go about your normal activities, but do not let it distract you from the core GregTech progression. The magic path becomes more important and more accessible once you have stable food, shelter, and basic ore processing established. Save dedicated Thaumcraft sessions for after you have your first steam machines running.
