GT New Horizons (GTNH) is one of the most ambitious modpacks ever made for Minecraft. Built on Minecraft 1.7.10 and centered on the GregTech mod, it weaves together over 200 mods into a single cohesive experience that can take months — or even years — to complete. More than a decade of active development has produced thousands of recipe tweaks, custom mods coded specifically for the pack, a questbook with over 3500 guided quests, unique world generation, and custom Thaumonomicon pages. The result feels less like a collection of mods bolted together and more like a standalone game.

## Key features

- **GregTech tech tree** — A tiered progression from stone-age hand tools through steam power, low-voltage electricity, and eventually exotic matter processing. Every technology tier unlocks new machines, materials, and automation options.
- **3500+ quest questbook** — BetterQuesting guides you through every major system. Quest text explains what to do and why, quest items are clickable to open NEI recipes, and completing quests rewards loot bags and coins.
- **Magic integration** — Four major magic mods (Thaumcraft, Blood Magic, Botania, Witchery) are tied together by a custom Thauminomicon and gated behind meaningful progression milestones.
- **Ore vein system** — GregTech replaces random ore scatter with large localized veins. Finding a vein rewards you with enormous quantities of a specific material, encouraging exploration across dimensions.
- **Space progression** — Galacticraft and Galaxy Space send you to the Moon, Mars, and beyond to gather resources only available off-world. Space travel is a required step toward the endgame, not an optional side activity.
- **Survival challenge** — Hardcore darkness, Spice of Life food diversity requirements, Infernal Mobs, and Pam's Harvestcraft make survival genuinely difficult in the early game.
- **200+ integrated mods** — Every mod in the pack has been carefully balanced against the others. Recipes are tweaked, progression gates are enforced, and content is distributed across the tech tiers so the pack feels unified.
- **Active development** — GTNH has been in continuous development for over 10 years and is still actively maintained, with regular releases and a large contributor community.

<Note>
  GTNH is not a casual playthrough. A typical run to endgame takes many months of regular play, and some players measure their progress in years. If you are looking for a quick weekend modpack, this is not it. If you want a deep, rewarding challenge that keeps giving, read on.
</Note>
