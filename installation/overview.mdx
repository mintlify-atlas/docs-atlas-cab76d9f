GT New Horizons is a large, highly customized modpack that requires a compatible launcher to install correctly. The pack bundles hundreds of mods, custom configurations, and scripts that must all be present together — you cannot assemble it by downloading mods one by one. This page walks you through the full installation process from start to finish.

<Note>
  Do not download mods individually from the modlist — it is provided for reference only. Always use the official pack download to get a complete, working installation.
</Note>

## Installation steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Choose a launcher">
    Select a modpack launcher that supports custom modpacks. Prism Launcher and MultiMC are the most recommended options. See [Launcher Options](/installation/launchers) for a full comparison.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check Java">
    GT New Horizons requires Java 8, 17, or 21. Java 17 is recommended for the best balance of compatibility and performance. See [Java Requirements](/installation/java-requirements) for details and setup instructions.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Download the pack">
    Download the latest release from the official downloads page at [gtnewhorizons.com/downloads](https://www.gtnewhorizons.com/downloads/). Import the downloaded pack file into your launcher of choice.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure RAM">
    Open your launcher's instance settings and allocate at least 6 GB of RAM to the GTNH instance. 8 GB or more is recommended for stable gameplay, especially on larger worlds.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Launch">
    Start the instance from your launcher. The first launch will take longer than usual as assets are loaded and cached. Once the main menu appears, you are ready to play.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Allocate 6–8 GB of RAM as a minimum. Players on larger worlds or servers with many loaded chunks should target 8–10 GB. Too little RAM is the most common cause of lag and out-of-memory crashes.
</Tip>
