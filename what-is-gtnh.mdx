GT New Horizons is described by its creators as "a big progressive kitchensink pack for Minecraft 1.7.10 balanced around the mod GregTech." That sentence rewards unpacking. It is kitchensink in the sense that it includes an enormous range of mods — over 200 — covering technology, magic, exploration, storage, agriculture, genetics, computers, and more. It is progressive and balanced in the sense that none of those mods are freely available from the start. Each system is gated behind earlier progression, carefully integrated with the GregTech tech tree, and tuned through thousands of recipe tweaks so that everything feels like it belongs to the same game. The result, after more than a decade of active development, is one of the most polished and demanding modpacks ever made.

## Technology

GregTech is the backbone of the pack. It introduces the EU (Energy Unit) power system and divides progression into a series of technology tiers — Ultra Low Voltage (ULV), Low Voltage (LV), Medium Voltage (MV), High Voltage (HV), and beyond — each representing a new age of machinery and materials. You do not simply craft a machine and move on; you craft it, build the infrastructure to power it, find or process the specific materials it requires, and integrate it into a larger processing line.

Complex multi-step ore processing is one of GregTech's defining features. A single ore can be crushed, washed, centrifuged, smelted in a blast furnace, and cooled in a vacuum freezer, each step increasing the yield of the final material. Early in the pack this is done by hand or with simple machines; by mid-game you are designing automated processing lines to handle dozens of material streams simultaneously.

The main GregTech cables convert EU to RF, so you do not have to manage incompatible energy systems between mods. Technology mods become available as you reach the appropriate tier:

- **IndustrialCraft 2** — Electric tools, crop crossbreeding, nuclear reactors, and the foundation of EU-based power.
- **BuildCraft** — Pipes, quarries, and fluid handling that integrates cleanly with GregTech processing.
- **Applied Energistics 2** — Digital item and fluid storage, auto-crafting, and ME networks that become essential infrastructure in the mid-to-late game.
- **EnderIO** — Conduits, mob farming, and the soul-bound enchantment system.
- **Railcraft** — Rail networks and tanks for large-scale logistics.
- **Forestry** — Bee breeding and tree farming for automating biological resource production.
- **Draconic Evolution** — Endgame power generation and storage.

## Magic

GTNH treats magic as a parallel progression path rather than a throwaway add-on. The Thauminomicon — normally the research book for Thaumcraft — is extended with custom sections for Botania, Blood Magic, Witchery, and other magic systems, giving magic a unified entry point and research tree.

The Twilight Forest is the dimension linked to magic. You must explore it, defeat its bosses, and claim their power to advance magic progression. Later in the game, magic and technology converge: essentia can power AE systems, magical research unlocks new machine recipes, and hybrid tools from Electro Magic Tools bridge both worlds.

The four main magic mods each offer a distinct playstyle:

- **Thaumcraft** — Aspect research, wand crafting, golems, infusion altars, and the constant management of warp and taint.
- **Blood Magic** — Sacrifice-based magic requiring a blood altar, life points, and increasingly complex rituals to unlock powerful spells and tools.
- **Botania** — Flower-powered mana generation with a self-contained tech tree of functional flowers, runes, and elven trade recipes.
- **Witchery** — Ritual circles, cauldron brews, familiars, and a deep system of pacts and curses with strong narrative flavor.

## Exploration

The overworld is generated by Realistic World Gen combined with Biomes O' Plenty, producing large, varied biomes with realistic terrain. Early villages provide shelter and a source of trades; later you may recruit villagers back to your base or take over a village entirely.

GregTech's ore vein system replaces random ore scatter with large, localized deposits. A given chunk in the overworld may have no ore at all, but when you find a vein it yields hundreds of ore blocks of a specific material — enough to sustain a significant phase of your base's growth. This design encourages long exploration trips and rewards patience.

Not every resource is available in the overworld. GTNH explicitly requires you to mine in other dimensions:

- **The Nether** — Unique ores and materials unavailable in the overworld.
- **The End** — Additional resources and the Hardcore Ender Expansion boss fights.
- **Twilight Forest** — Magic-linked materials and boss loot.
- **Galacticraft / Galaxy Space** — Moon dust, Mars ore, and planet-specific materials that unlock endgame recipes. Space travel transitions from optional to mandatory as you approach the late game.

Roguelike Dungeons provide challenging combat encounters with meaningful loot, and mob farms built with EnderIO and Draconic Evolution become important sources of materials at higher tiers.

## Questing

The questbook is powered by BetterQuesting and contains over 3500 quests. It is not decorative — it is the primary guide through the entire pack. Quest descriptions explain why a system exists, what you need to do next, and how different mods connect to each other. You do not need external tutorials or wikis to understand most systems because the questbook covers them.

Practical features make the questbook especially useful:

- **In-line NEI integration** — Clicking any item shown in a quest opens its recipe in Not Enough Items immediately, saving you time at the NEI search interface.
- **Loot bags** — Quest rewards include tiered loot bags with randomized contents, adding a pleasant element of chance to progression rewards.
- **Coin system** — Some quests reward coins that can be spent on specific items, providing a deterministic way to obtain certain materials when luck has not been kind.
- **Guided transitions** — Quests flag the exact moment you should move to a new tech tier, start a new mod system, or begin building new infrastructure.

## Survival challenge

The first few nights are genuinely dangerous. Hardcore Darkness makes unlit areas pitch black — not dimly lit but completely unnavigable without a light source or potion effect. Infernal Mobs adds rare, enhanced variants with unpredictable abilities. SpecialMobs and EnderZoo introduce new enemy types that require actual combat strategy to defeat.

Food variety is enforced by Spice of Life. Eating the same food repeatedly reduces the hunger it restores, so you cannot survive on bread alone. Pam's Harvestcraft provides hundreds of crops and recipes, and setting up a diverse farm becomes a meaningful early goal rather than an afterthought.

## Who is this pack for?

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="I'm new to Minecraft. Can I play GTNH?">
    No. GTNH assumes you understand basic Minecraft mechanics — crafting, farming, combat, the Nether — before it begins teaching you modded systems. Start with vanilla Minecraft or a beginner-friendly modpack first.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="I've played modded Minecraft before. Is this too hard?">
    If you have played other expert packs (SevTech Ages, Omnifactory, Enigmatica 6 Expert), you are ready for GTNH. It is harder than most and longer than all of them, but the questbook guides you through every system and the learning curve is manageable with patience.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How long does a full playthrough take?">
    A typical run to the current endgame takes many months of regular play, often exceeding a year. The pack is intentionally long. Many players never reach the true endgame and still find the journey rewarding.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="I want to reach endgame fast. Is there a shortcut?">
    There is not, by design. The pack is balanced around gradual progression through every tier. Attempts to skip tiers are blocked by recipe requirements, and the satisfaction of the pack comes from earning each stage rather than bypassing it.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I play with friends?">
    Yes. Multiplayer is fully supported. There are official GTNH servers, whitelisted community servers, and open servers available. See the [Discord](https://discord.gg/gtnh) for current server listings and application instructions.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Do I need to know GregTech already?">
    No. The questbook introduces GregTech from the very beginning. Prior experience helps, but it is not required. The pack explains what machines do, why processing lines work the way they do, and when to build each new system.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
