Botania is a flower-powered magic mod with a philosophy distinct from every other magic system in GT New Horizons: it is almost entirely self-contained, with a tight internal economy built around generating and spending Mana. Where Thaumcraft demands aspect scanning and research unlocks and Blood Magic requires infrastructure and sacrifice automation, Botania rewards careful reading and creative engineering. The mod's progression flows naturally from its own in-game book, and it can be advanced largely in parallel with other systems rather than being blocked behind them.

<Note>
  Botania is more self-contained than other magic mods in GTNH — you can progress through much of it independently without needing to unlock other magic systems first. This makes it a good early investment alongside your GregTech work.
</Note>

<Tip>
  Read the Lexica Botania carefully — it is one of the best in-game documentation books in any Minecraft mod. Every mechanic, recipe, and flower is explained clearly with diagrams. Keep it in your hotbar throughout your Botania progression.
</Tip>

## Mana system

Mana is the single resource that drives all of Botania. It is generated by functional flowers called Generating Flowers, stored in Mana Pools, and transported between blocks by Mana Spreaders. Unlike EU or RF, Mana is not a streaming energy — it moves in discrete pulses called Mana Bursts fired by Spreaders. A properly designed Mana network routes bursts efficiently from generators to pools and then to the flowers or devices that consume it.

## Generating flowers

Generating flowers consume some kind of real-world input and convert it into Mana.

- **Endoflame** — burns coal, charcoal, or any solid fuel. The simplest flower to start with.
- **Hydroangeas** — consumes adjacent water source blocks passively. Low output but zero maintenance.
- **Kekimurus** — eats cake blocks placed nearby. Reliable mid-game option once you have cake automation.
- **Gourmaryllis** — gains efficiency by receiving a variety of different foods in sequence. Higher variety means higher output, making food automation from Pam's Harvestcraft a natural pairing.

In GTNH, some generating flowers can interact with GregTech machine outputs. Steam and certain chemical outputs from GregTech processing can feed flowers that accept fluid or chemical inputs, allowing you to tie Botania generation into your existing tech lines.

## Functional flowers

Functional flowers consume Mana to perform work in the world.

- **Agricarnation** — pulses growth ticks to nearby crops, accelerating farms without any player input
- **Bellethorne** — damages hostile mobs in range, useful for passive defense around your base
- **Daffomill** — blows nearby items in a direction, acting as a simple item transport mechanism
- **Hopperhock** — collects nearby items into an adjacent inventory, replacing hoppers in many setups
- **Rannuncarpus** — places blocks from an adjacent inventory, enabling automated building and farm construction

## Mana infrastructure

Moving Mana from generators to consumers requires a network of Spreaders, Pools, and lenses.

- **Mana Spreaders** — fire Mana Bursts toward a bound target. Range and efficiency vary by material tier (Mana, Elven, Gaia).
- **Mana Pools** — store Mana and act as the central buffers in any network. Required for most crafting operations.
- **Mana Detectors** — output a redstone signal proportional to Mana flow, useful for automation and monitoring.
- **Mana Void** — safely deletes excess Mana to prevent overflow in a network.
- **Mana Lenses** — modify Spreader burst behavior (splitting, bouncing, painting, boosting efficiency).

## Key Botania items

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Terrasteel">
    Terrasteel is the primary mid-game crafting material in Botania. It is created by placing a Manasteel Ingot, a Mana Diamond, and a Mana Pearl simultaneously on a Mana Pool with sufficient Mana present. The three items slowly absorb Mana until they combine into a Terrasteel Ingot. Terrasteel tools and armor are strong in their own right, but Terrasteel is also a key ingredient in Elementium crafting and in cross-mod GTNH recipes that bridge Botania into GregTech's material system.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Elementium and Pixie dust">
    Elementium is crafted in the Elven Trade system by sending materials through an Alfheim Portal. It requires establishing a stable portal connection to Alfheim, which itself demands Terrasteel and Mana investment to build and maintain. Elementium armor has the special property of summoning Pixies to defend you when you take damage — a passive combat effect that scales with how many pieces of the set you wear.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Runes and mana equipment">
    Runes (Fire, Water, Earth, Air, Spring, Summer, Autumn, Winter) are crafted on a Runic Altar and serve as the intermediate ingredients for nearly all high-tier Botania items. Each rune requires Mana and specific material inputs. Mana equipment — Manasteel tools and armor — provides the first upgrade tier above vanilla gear and uses Mana from a Mana Tablet in your inventory to repair itself automatically during use.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Botanic Horizons addon

GTNH ships the Botanic Horizons addon, which adds additional Botania content beyond the base mod. This includes new flowers, additional mana-powered devices, and extended progression content that integrates with GTNH-specific systems. Check the Thauminomicon's Botania section for details on what Botanic Horizons adds and where it fits in the progression sequence.

## GTNH integration

Botania does not exist in isolation in GTNH. Several cross-mod interaction points connect it to GregTech:

- **Mana to EU conversion** — dedicated machines allow Mana stored in Mana Pools to be converted into EU for GregTech machines, making a large Botania operation a legitimate supplemental power source.
- **GregTech materials in Botania recipes** — some GTNH-modified Botania recipes require GregTech alloys or processed materials as ingredients, gating certain items behind your tech progression.
- **Petal Apothecary integration** — certain GregTech-processed chemical outputs can substitute for vanilla ingredients in the Petal Apothecary, providing alternative crafting paths.

These integrations make Botania a system worth developing thoroughly rather than treating as an optional side project — the Mana-to-EU conversion alone can meaningfully contribute to your power budget in the mid-game.
