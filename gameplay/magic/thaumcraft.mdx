Thaumcraft is the primary magic mod in GT New Horizons and serves as the gateway to the entire magic progression system. GTNH deeply integrates Thaumcraft into its tech tree, adding custom Thaumonomicon pages, cross-mod recipes, and unique research unlocks that tie magic advancement directly to GregTech tiers. You cannot simply rush through it — magic in GTNH is gated behind the Twilight Forest dimension, and meaningful progress requires defeating its bosses before many research branches open up.

## The Thauminomicon

GTNH replaces the standard Thaumonomicon with the **Thauminomicon**, a modified version that includes custom sections for all magic mods in the pack. In addition to the usual Thaumcraft content and its addons, you will find dedicated tabs for Botania, Blood Magic, Witchery, and GTNH-specific content. This makes the Thauminomicon your single reference point for the entire magic system rather than needing a separate book for each mod.

<Note>
  The Thauminomicon Introduction tab in-game explains all GTNH-specific content and customizations — read it before diving into any magic progression.
</Note>

## Aspects system

Aspects are the fundamental units of Thaumcraft's magic. Every item in the game has one or more aspects associated with it, and you discover these by scanning items with a Thaumometer. The primary aspects include Ignis (fire), Aqua (water), Terra (earth), Aer (air), Ordo (order), and Perditio (entropy). These combine into compound aspects like Lux, Motus, Machina, and many others.

Scanning items is not optional — it is how you unlock research entries. Building a broad aspect library early means less grinding later when advanced recipes demand rare compound aspects.

## Research mechanics

Research is unlocked through a minigame played at a Research Table. You place two aspects on the board and draw connections between them using intermediate aspects. Successfully linking aspects completes a research topic and unlocks new crafting recipes or abilities. Some research requires items or essentia deposited into an Arcane Worktable to actually craft the resulting items.

GTNH includes the **thaumcraft-research-tweaks** mod, which adjusts research difficulty and requirements to better fit the pack's progression pace.

## Vis and Warp system

Vis is the magical energy that powers wands and certain Thaumcraft machines. It is drawn from aura nodes scattered throughout the world and stored in wands or staves. Managing your local node network matters — draining nodes too aggressively reduces the ambient vis in a chunk and slows your ability to craft.

Warp is the cost of pushing too far into dangerous research and using dark magic items. It accumulates in three forms: temporary, permanent, and sticky warp. Higher warp levels trigger increasingly disruptive effects: hallucinations, hostile Eldritch Guardians spawning nearby, and eventually permanent debuffs.

<Warning>
  Warp causes increasingly dangerous effects — manage it carefully. Avoid researching forbidden topics until you are prepared to deal with the consequences, and keep your warp level low by using Balanced Shards and safe research paths.
</Warning>

## The Twilight Forest connection

Magic progression in GTNH is explicitly gated behind the Twilight Forest. Several important Thaumcraft research branches and high-tier recipes require boss drops or items only obtainable from Twilight Forest bosses. You will need to explore and defeat the Naga, Lich, Minoshroom, Hydra, Ur-ghast, and Snow Queen before the full depth of the magic system becomes accessible.

## Thaumcraft addons in GTNH

GTNH ships a large suite of Thaumcraft addons, each extending the base mod in a different direction.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Forbidden Magic">
    Forbidden Magic adds dark and demonic Thaumcraft content. It introduces cross-mod aspects drawn from other magic mods (Blood Magic, Witchery, Botania), letting you scan items from those mods to discover new aspects. It also adds powerful but warp-heavy items including Eldritch weapons and dark infusion recipes. Expect significant warp accumulation when using Forbidden Magic content.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Thaumic Tinkerer">
    Thaumic Tinkerer provides high-tier Thaumcraft gear and quality-of-life items. Notable additions include the Ichorium tools and armor (extremely powerful late-game equipment), the KAMI suite of endgame Thaumcraft items, and various utility blocks that streamline research and essentia management. Most KAMI items require both significant Thaumcraft research completion and high-tier GregTech materials.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Thaumic Horizons">
    Thaumic Horizons focuses on deeper automation of Thaumcraft processes. It adds golem-related automation improvements, new infusion recipes, and tools for managing large-scale essentia logistics. Useful once you are producing essentia in bulk for power generation or crafting.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Thaumic Energistics">
    Thaumic Energistics bridges Thaumcraft and Applied Energistics 2, allowing essentia to be stored in your ME network as if it were a fluid or item. Essentia Terminals, Essentia Cells, and Essentia Export/Import Buses let you build a fully integrated magic-tech storage system. This is one of the most useful late-game integrations in GTNH, making large-scale Thaumcraft automation far more manageable.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Nodal Mechanics">
    Nodal Mechanics allows you to manipulate, move, and modify aura nodes. You can relocate nodes to consolidate vis sources near your base, merge nodes to create more powerful ones, or carefully split large nodes. Node management becomes important once you are running multiple essentia-hungry machines.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Tainted Magic">
    Tainted Magic adds a progression path built around embracing warp rather than avoiding it. It introduces warp-based items and mechanics that are powerful precisely because they carry heavy warp costs. If you want to deliberately pursue a high-warp playstyle, this mod provides meaningful rewards for doing so.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Late game: essentia-powered generation

At the end of the Thaumcraft progression, essentia production becomes a legitimate power source. Specialized machines can convert essentia into EU, connecting the magic system to GregTech's energy network. This is the payoff for building out your Thaumcraft infrastructure — essentia from a large, automated operation can provide meaningful power generation that complements your GregTech setups.
