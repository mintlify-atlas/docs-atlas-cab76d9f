Blood Magic is a life-powered magic system where your own health — and eventually the health of mobs — is your primary resource. In GT New Horizons, Blood Magic is integrated into the overall magic progression and tied to the Thauminomicon, with cross-mod recipes requiring GregTech materials and Thaumcraft aspects at higher tiers. The system rewards patience and infrastructure investment: a well-built Blood Magic setup practically runs itself once you have mob sacrifice automation in place.

<Warning>
  Sacrificing too much health can kill you — keep healing items handy. Never drain yourself below a safe threshold, especially early on when your altar tier limits how efficiently you generate Life Points.
</Warning>

## Life Essence (LP) system

Life Essence (LP) is the currency of Blood Magic. You generate it by sacrificing your own health at a Blood Altar using the Sacrificial Knife, or by sacrificing mobs on a Mob Altar using the Dagger of Sacrifice. LP fills your personal Soul Network, which acts as the reservoir that powers all Blood Magic items and rituals. Your Soul Network has a maximum capacity that increases as you upgrade your altar tier and craft stronger binding crystals.

## Blood Altar tiers

The Blood Altar is the central crafting station of Blood Magic. Upgrading it requires surrounding it with specific rune combinations and block types. Each tier unlocks new crafting recipes and increases the maximum LP your altar can hold.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Tier 1 — First blood">
    The base altar requires only the altar block itself. At Tier 1 you can craft the Bound Blade, basic Sigils (Water Sigil, Lava Sigil), and a Sacrificial Orb. Self-sacrifice with the Sacrificial Knife is your only LP source at this stage. Craft Blank Slates to begin unlocking higher tiers.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Tier 2 — Rune expansion">
    Surround the altar with Runes of Self-Sacrifice. Tier 2 increases altar capacity and enables better sigils. You can now craft a Dagger of Sacrifice to begin mob sacrifice, though a proper mob farm is more valuable than random kills.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Tier 3 — Mob sacrifice">
    Add Runes of Sacrifice and glowstone blocks to the structure. Tier 3 opens access to the Sentient Sword — a weapon that grows more powerful as Demon Will crystals fill it — and more capable sigils. This is when a dedicated mob sacrifice setup pays off significantly.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Tier 4 — Demon will and the demon realm">
    Requires Large Blood Stone Bricks as pillar caps. Tier 4 unlocks Demon Will Crystal crafting and access to the Demon Realm dimension. Demon Will is a secondary resource collected by killing mobs with the Sentient Sword; it powers the most advanced Blood Magic tools and rituals.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Tier 5 — High-tier sigils and summoning">
    Adds Bloody Void pillars (a GTNH custom material). Tier 5 enables the highest-tier sigils and demon summoning rituals. At this stage Blood Magic starts to feel genuinely powerful, with passive effects that meaningfully change how you play.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Tier 6 — Endgame altar">
    Requires Bloody Ichorium pillars and Crystal blocks as caps — both demand deep GregTech and Blood Magic progression to obtain. Tier 6 unlocks the final tier of Blood Magic items and the most powerful rituals available. Reaching this point represents a major milestone in your GTNH playthrough.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Set up a mob farm early to automate LP generation through mob sacrifice. A simple spawner farm with a Dagger of Sacrifice mechanism provides far more LP per hour than self-sacrifice alone, and frees you from the health management juggling act entirely.
</Tip>

## Sigils

Sigils are passive-effect items held in your hand or bound to your hotbar. They consume LP from your Soul Network to provide ongoing utility effects.

- **Water Sigil** — places water source blocks
- **Lava Sigil** — places lava source blocks
- **Green Grove Sigil** — accelerates crop growth in the area
- **Sigil of the Dome** — creates a temporary air bubble underwater
- **Sigil of Haste** — provides a speed boost
- **Sigil of Elemental Affinity** — grants fire and fall damage immunity

Higher-tier sigils require more LP to activate and more complex crafting materials, but provide correspondingly stronger effects.

## Rituals

Rituals are large multi-block structures built from Ritual Stones in specific patterns on the ground. When activated with a Master Ritual Stone and sufficient LP, they produce powerful ongoing effects. Examples include:

- **Ritual of the Green Grove** — large-area crop acceleration
- **Ritual of Magnetism** — draws ore veins up from underground
- **Ritual of the Crusher** — processes ores at the ritual site
- **Ritual of Teleposition** — teleports players or entities between linked altars
- **Ritual of Containment** — traps mobs in an area for passive sacrifice

Rituals consume LP continuously while active. Make sure your Soul Network can sustain whichever rituals you run simultaneously.

## BloodArsenal addon

BloodArsenal extends Blood Magic with additional items and armor sets. It adds Blood Infused Iron and Blood Infused Glowstone as crafting materials, new armor tiers that use Blood Magic mechanics, and additional tools that tie into the LP system. The Blood-Infused Glowstone is specifically used as a Tier 3 altar cap alternative in GTNH's Blood Altar configuration.

## Integration with Thaumcraft

Blood Magic and Thaumcraft interact through Forbidden Magic, which adds Blood Magic-specific aspects (Vitium, Exsanguinate) that appear when you scan Blood Magic items with a Thaumometer. Several Thaumcraft infusion recipes use Blood Magic components as ingredients, and some high-tier Blood Magic crafting requires Thaumcraft materials in return. The Thauminomicon contains a dedicated Blood Magic section documenting these cross-mod recipes.
