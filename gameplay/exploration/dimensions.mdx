---
title: "Dimensions and Other Worlds in GTNH"
description: "GTNH uses multiple dimensions as progression gates, each containing unique resources and challenges required to advance through the technology tiers."
---

GT New Horizons is not a single-world game. Progression through the pack requires visiting other dimensions to obtain materials that simply do not exist in the overworld. Each dimension opens at a different point in the technology ladder, and each one contains enemies, environmental hazards, and resource deposits calibrated to match where you should be when you first arrive. The overworld is where you start; other worlds are where you grow.

<Warning>
Each new dimension has increasingly difficult mobs and environmental hazards. Enter underprepared and you will die quickly. Check the questbook for recommended gear before making your first visit to any dimension.
</Warning>

## Quick reference

| Dimension | When to visit | Key resources |
|-----------|--------------|---------------|
| The Nether | Early game (after basic armour) | Nether Quartz, Blaze Rods, Nether ores |
| The End | Mid-game (post-EBF) | Dragon Egg, Endstone ores, HEE content |
| Twilight Forest | Early–mid game | Magic progression materials, boss loot |
| The Deep Dark | Mid-game | Rich ore deposits, dangerous mobs |
| Moon | Mid-game (Galacticraft) | Ilmenite, Olivine, Aluminium |
| Mars | Late-game | Tetrahedrite, Magnesium, space ores |
| Asteroids | Late-game | Rare space ores, loot chests |
| Galaxy Space planets | Endgame | Extreme-tier materials |
| Amun-Ra | Endgame | Additional space dimension resources |

## The Nether

The Nether is the first dimension most players visit, accessible as soon as you have a flint and steel and enough obsidian. GregTech adds many ore types to the Nether beyond what vanilla provides:

- **Nether Quartz veins** — an essential early material for circuits and electronic components
- **Sulfur** — used in chemical processing chains
- **Netherrack-based ores** — various GregTech metals that generate only in the Nether
- **Blaze Rods** — required for multiple mid-game crafting recipes and magic progression

The Nether is also the most efficient travel medium in the early game. One block of Nether distance equals eight blocks of overworld distance, making a Nether road system a major quality-of-life investment.

## The End

The End contains the Ender Dragon, which must be defeated to fully access this dimension. **Hardcore Ender Expansion** is included in GTNH and significantly increases the difficulty and content of The End:

- The Ender Dragon fight is harder than vanilla
- **Endstone ores** provide unique materials not found elsewhere
- The **Dragon Egg** is a critical crafting material for certain high-tier recipes
- Hardcore Ender Expansion adds additional structures and content beyond the vanilla void island

## The Twilight Forest

<Tip>
The Twilight Forest is accessible relatively early in the pack — prioritize it for magic progression. A diamond, a bucket of water, and some flowers are all you need to open the portal.
</Tip>

The Twilight Forest is the magic dimension in GTNH. It is a perpetually twilight world with dense forests, unique biomes, and four major bosses that must be defeated in sequence to unlock the final area. Defeating all four bosses is required to complete the Twilight Forest progression:

1. **Naga** — the first boss, found in a hedge maze
2. **Lich** — towers throughout the forest
3. **Hydra** — a massive multi-headed boss in a hollow hill
4. **Ur-Ghast** — a flying boss in the Dark Tower

The Twilight Forest provides unique wood types, magical materials, and boss loot that feeds into Thaumcraft gating. You cannot fully progress in the magic side of GTNH without completing the Twilight Forest sequence.

## The Deep Dark

The Deep Dark is an Extra Utilities dimension with permanent night, aggressive mobs, and no natural light. It is dangerous but contains **rich ore deposits** that make the risk worthwhile:

- Ore density is significantly higher than the overworld
- The permanent darkness and dangerous mob spawns require strong armour and lighting solutions before mining
- Best approached after you have reliable armour and healing

## Galacticraft dimensions

Galacticraft introduces space travel to GTNH, and reaching space is a major progression milestone. Space suits are mandatory — without them, you will suffocate and die in the vacuum.

### Moon

The Moon is the first space destination. Reaching it requires:

- A Tier 1 Galacticraft rocket
- Oxygen supplies (oxygen tanks and oxygen sealer for your base)
- A space suit

The Moon contains ore veins that do not exist in the overworld:

- **Ilmenite** — a titanium-iron ore exclusive to the Moon and asteroids
- **Olivine** — a mineral with uses in various GregTech recipes
- **Aluminium** — also available in space-specific veins
- **Cheese** — yes, the Moon surface contains cheese deposits

### Mars

Mars requires a Tier 2 rocket and a pressure suit to survive the atmospheric conditions. The ores found on Mars differ from both the overworld and Moon:

- **Tetrahedrite** — a copper-antimony mineral used in certain recipe chains
- **Magnesium** — required for advanced materials
- Various unique space ores not found elsewhere

Note that vanilla Galacticraft ore generation for iron, copper, tin, and Desh is disabled in GTNH (the pack uses GregTech ore veins for those materials instead).

### Asteroids

Asteroids (Tier 3 Galacticraft) provide additional iron, aluminium, and Ilmenite in space-specific vein distributions, along with loot chests from the GalacticGreg dungeon system.

## Galaxy Space

Galaxy Space extends the Galacticraft system with additional planets: **Venus, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune, Pluto, and Ceres**. These are extreme endgame destinations requiring progressively higher-tier rockets and planet-specific equipment. Each planet contains materials critical to the final stages of GTNH progression. Do not attempt Galaxy Space planets without thorough preparation — the environmental hazards scale well beyond Mars.

## Amun-Ra

Amun-Ra adds additional Galacticraft-compatible dimensions beyond the base Galaxy Space content. Like Galaxy Space planets, Amun-Ra is an endgame destination with unique resources tied to late-progression crafting chains.
