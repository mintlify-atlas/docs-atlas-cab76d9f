GT New Horizons transforms the Minecraft overworld into something worth exploring. The combination of Realistic World Gen and Biomes O' Plenty produces a world with continent-scale landmasses, proper river systems, varied climate zones, and over sixty distinct biomes — none of which resemble vanilla Minecraft terrain. Finding the right spot to build your base becomes a meaningful decision, and the world rewards extended exploration rather than punishing it.

## Realistic World Gen (RWG)

**Realistic World Gen** replaces vanilla terrain generation entirely. Instead of the flat noise-based hills of vanilla Minecraft, RWG produces:

- Continent-scale terrain with genuine mountains, valleys, and coastal regions
- Flowing rivers that follow geography, classified by climate (ice, cold, temperate, hot, wet)
- Oceans that vary by temperature zone
- Volcanic terrain with active lava flows using Biomes O' Plenty ash stone
- Cobblestone boulders and ravines scattered naturally across the landscape

The climate distance in GTNH is configured at 1600 blocks, meaning climate zones shift gradually over large distances rather than changing abruptly. Temperature and humidity determine which biomes appear, so travelling far enough in a consistent direction will reliably transition you from cold forests to hot deserts.

## Biomes O' Plenty

**Biomes O' Plenty** adds over 60 new biomes on top of what RWG generates. Each biome has distinct surface blocks, flora, and terrain features — tropical islands with palm trees, redwood forests with enormous trunks, oases surrounded by desert, and ash wastelands near volcanoes. These biomes are not cosmetic additions; some produce unique wood types, plants, and soils that are used in crafting recipes across the pack.

## Structures

### Villages

Villages are early-game safe havens. When you first spawn and spend your first few nights surviving, stumbling across a village provides shelter, light sources, and a chance to recover. As you progress, villages become resource nodes — villagers can be traded with for key items, and eventually you may find yourself relocating villagers to your base to support trade-based progression routes.

### Rogue-like dungeons

Rogue-like Dungeons are multi-floor structures scattered throughout the world, filled with mob spawners and increasingly dangerous rooms as you descend. They contain loot chests with materials and equipment that can accelerate early-game progression. The spawners inside are also valuable — capturing and relocating them enables automated mob farms later in the game.

## GregTech ore vein system

GregTech does not scatter individual ore blocks throughout the world. Instead, **all ores spawn in large, localised veins** — 3D clusters concentrated in a single area, with a primary ore type, one or more secondary ore types, and occasional bonus ores.

- A single vein can supply your base with a particular material for a long time once found
- Veins are spaced across the world rather than randomly distributed, which means you must search a larger area than vanilla mining to locate them
- Finding a vein feels genuinely rewarding because of the resources it provides

Because vanilla ore generation is disabled in GTNH (confirmed in the RTG config — coal, iron, gold, diamond, lapis, and redstone do not generate through the world generator), **GregTech veins are the only source of ore in the overworld**. This is not a limitation — it is how the pack is designed.

## Surface ores

Small ore deposits appear on the surface across the world. These surface ore nodes are critical for early-game material gathering before you have the tools and knowledge to locate underground veins. They also serve as **vein indicators**: a surface ore of a given type suggests a full vein may exist beneath that chunk.

<Tip>
JourneyMap is included in GTNH and runs automatically. Use it to map explored territory and place waypoints on surface ore locations while you explore — it makes returning to promising sites much easier.
</Tip>

<Note>
The VisualProspecting mod helps you track ore vein locations on your JourneyMap. When you find a vein, VisualProspecting records it and displays it on your map so you never lose track of a deposit.
</Note>

    Choosing where to settle matters more in GTNH than in most modpacks. Consider the following when scouting:

    - **Flat terrain near a river** makes early construction easier and provides a water source without digging
    - **Temperate forest biomes** give early access to diverse wood types and moderate mob difficulty at night
    - **Avoid deep ocean spawns** — open ocean has little to offer in the early game and is difficult to navigate without tools
    - **Check for villages nearby** — a village within a few hundred blocks is a significant early-game advantage
    - **Note biome transitions** — setting up near a biome border gives you access to resources from multiple biomes without long travel
    - **Mark your spawn** with a JourneyMap waypoint the moment you load in — losing your starting position in RWG terrain is disorienting
    - Once you have access to a Nether portal, the Nether highway system (travelling in the Nether to cover 8× the overworld distance) makes base location less critical for long-distance travel
