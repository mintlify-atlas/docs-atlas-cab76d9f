title: "Electric Age: LV Through EV Tier-by-Tier Progression"

The transition from steam to electricity is one of the most significant shifts in GT New Horizons. Steam machines gave you ore doubling and basic processing; electric machines give you ore quadrupling, chemical processing, and access to materials impossible to produce with fire and bellows alone. The Electric Age spans four major voltage tiers — LV, MV, HV, and EV — each of which unlocks new machines, multiblocks, and complexity that the previous tier could not support. The arc from your first Coal Generator to industrial-scale processing lines takes months of real time.

<Note>
Each tier requires the previous tier's circuits and materials — you cannot skip tiers. An MV machine requires LV-tier circuits in its recipe; an HV machine requires MV circuits. There are no shortcuts.
</Note>

## LV — Low Voltage (8 EU/t)

LV is where electricity begins. Steel production from the Steam Age unlocks LV machine casings, and from there you can build the first wave of electric single-block machines.

**First electric machines:**
- **LV Macerator** — ore grinding, now faster and quieter than the steam version
- **LV Centrifuge** — separates mixed dusts and materials into components
- **LV Chemical Reactor** — the first access to chemical processing recipes
- **LV Electrolyzer** — breaks down chemical compounds into elements

**First generators:**
- **Coal Generator** — burns coal and coal coke for 8 EU/t; your first source of EU
- **Wind Mill** — passive generation dependent on height and biome; unreliable but free
- **Water Mill** — passive generation dependent on adjacent water; very low output

Circuit crafting begins at LV. **Basic Electronic Circuits** are required for nearly every machine you will build from this point forward, and the circuit recipes grow progressively more complex at each tier. Set up circuit production early and keep it running.

**Applied Energistics 2** becomes accessible at LV. Building your ME network now — even a minimal setup — pays dividends immediately. ME drives and interfaces make material sorting and retrieval far faster than chests and hoppers.

## MV — Medium Voltage (32 EU/t)

MV's defining machine is the **Electric Blast Furnace (EBF)**, a multiblock structure required to smelt aluminium, advanced steel alloys, and most metals beyond iron. Without the EBF, you cannot progress — it is a hard gate on mid-game materials.

**Key machines:**
- **Electric Blast Furnace (EBF)** — required for all high-temperature smelting; runs on coil blocks rated for specific heat levels
- **Chemical Bath** — processes materials with fluid inputs; unlocks a range of chemical recipes
- **Thermal Centrifuge** — further processes crushed ore for byproduct recovery
- **Arc Furnace** — smelts materials in bulk at high efficiency

**Power generation:**
- **Medium Solar Panels** — MV-tier solar panels produce more EU/t than LV variants
- **MV Generator** — burns fuel to produce 32 EU/t

Forestry **bees** become genuinely useful at MV. Specific bee species produce resources directly — alloy dusts, rare materials, even circuit components — making apiaries a meaningful supplement to mining.

## HV — High Voltage (128 EU/t)

HV is when large multi-block machines become viable and the scale of your operation needs to grow significantly. Processing chains that were manageable at MV start requiring dedicated infrastructure.

**Key multiblocks:**
- **Large Chemical Reactor** — processes chemical recipes in bulk with higher efficiency than single-block variants
- **Distillation Tower** — separates crude oil and other fluids into component fractions; essential for plastic and petrochemical production
- **Vacuum Freezer** — required for cooling hot ingots produced by the EBF

**Circuits and components** advance to Good Electronic Circuits and beyond, requiring more diverse input materials and more processing steps. Automation becomes not just convenient but necessary — hand-crafting HV circuits is impractical at volume.

**AE2 networks** can scale up significantly at HV. P2P tunnels, larger drive bays, and more sophisticated crafting patterns allow you to automate multi-step processing lines end-to-end.

The first **Galacticraft** rocket components become craftable at HV. You are not launching yet, but gathering the materials and building the infrastructure now shortens the path to EV.

## EV — Extreme Voltage (512 EU/t)

EV is the middle game — the tier where most players spend considerable time before the difficulty of IV and space becomes the next wall. Industrial-scale processing comes together at EV, and space exploration transitions from "possible eventually" to "actively required."

**Key capabilities:**
- Industrial-scale machines processing at throughputs that require dedicated input/output logistics
- Advanced circuit production chains with cleanroom requirements for higher-tier components
- Galacticraft launch infrastructure fully buildable — your first rocket is achievable at EV
- Endgame mod content begins unlocking, including late-stage Thaumcraft research and Blood Magic rituals

<Tip>
EV is the tier where players often pause to consolidate their base. Before pushing toward IV, ensure your AE2 network is handling all item logistics automatically, your ore processing chain runs without manual intervention, and you have a stable power supply well above your current consumption. IV's material requirements are demanding, and arriving underprepared causes significant delays.
</Tip>

<Warning>
Circuit complexity increases significantly at each tier. EV circuits require materials and processing steps that themselves require MV and HV machines to produce. Falling behind on circuit production stalls everything else. Automate your circuit production line before you need to scale it up — doing it reactively under pressure is much harder.
</Warning>

## Key machines by tier

| Tier | EU/t | Defining machines | Power sources |
|------|------|------------------|---------------|
| LV | 8 | Macerator, Centrifuge, Chemical Reactor | Coal Generator, Wind Mill |
| MV | 32 | Electric Blast Furnace, Thermal Centrifuge, Chemical Bath | Solar Panels, MV Generator |
| HV | 128 | Large Chemical Reactor, Distillation Tower, Vacuum Freezer | Large Turbines, HV Generator |
| EV | 512 | Assembly Machine, Canning Machine, advanced multiblocks | Diesel Generator, EV Solar |

## What comes next

Completing EV and preparing for IV requires resources from space. The Moon and Mars contain ores unavailable in the overworld, and those ores are required components in IV machine recipes. Space is not a side activity — it is the next tier's prerequisite.
