Space in GT New Horizons is not an optional adventure. It is a mandatory progression requirement. Many of the crafting materials needed for IV-tier machines and beyond exist exclusively on other planets — they do not generate in the overworld, the Nether, or the End. At a certain point in the Electric Age, the path forward is not deeper into your base but upward into orbit. Galacticraft and its companion mod Galaxy Space add dozens of planets and moons, each with unique atmospheric conditions, hostile environments, and exclusive ore deposits that drive progression all the way to UV tier.

## Why space matters

Several key materials required for IV and higher tier machines are only found off-world. This is not a soft suggestion to explore space for bonus resources — it is a hard gate. You cannot craft the components for IV machines without titanium and other space-exclusive ores. The questbook makes this explicit: specific quest chains will direct you to the Moon and Mars before IV content unlocks.

This design decision ties Galacticraft into the core progression loop in a way that many players initially find surprising. Galacticraft suddenly becomes essential, and the reward for completing your first rocket launch is access to a new layer of the tech tree that was previously unreachable.

## Galacticraft basics

Galacticraft adds the spacecraft, launch systems, and life support equipment needed to survive off-world. Before launching, you need to understand its requirements.

**Required equipment before any launch:**
- **Oxygen Gear** — provides breathable air in space and on airless bodies
- **Oxygen Tank** — stores oxygen supply; must be filled before departure
- **Space Suit** — protects against environmental hazards and vacuum exposure

These items require aluminium and other materials produced in the Electric Blast Furnace, which is why space becomes accessible in the HV–EV range rather than earlier.

**Rocket tiers:**
- **Tier 1 Rocket** — reaches the Moon; built from aluminium, steel, and rocket fuel
- **Tier 2 Rocket** — reaches Mars; requires additional materials and a Tier 2 launch pad
- **Tier 3+ Rockets** — reach the outer planets added by Galaxy Space

Rockets require a Launch Pad and Rocket Fuel to launch. The fuel itself is a GregTech processing product, tying launch logistics into your existing chemical processing infrastructure.

## Your first rocket launch

<Steps>
  <Step title="Secure aluminium and steel in quantity">
    The Tier 1 Rocket requires aluminium sheets, steel, and various components. Aluminium requires the Electric Blast Furnace to produce. Ensure you have a stable supply before starting rocket construction.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Craft and fill oxygen equipment">
    Craft Oxygen Gear, an Oxygen Tank, and a Space Suit. Fill the Oxygen Tank using an Oxygen Collector or Electrolyzer before launch — arriving on the Moon without oxygen is fatal within seconds.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Build and fuel the Launch Pad">
    Place a Launch Pad (a 3×3 platform of Launch Pad Blocks). Craft your Tier 1 Rocket and place it on the pad. Fill the fuel tank with Rocket Fuel using a fluid pipe connection.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Launch and navigate to the Moon">
    Right-click the rocket to board it, then activate the launch. A navigation screen allows you to select your destination. Choose the Moon for your first voyage.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Establish a base and mine">
    On arrival, set up an Oxygen Sealer to create a breathable area, or rely on your tank for short excursions. Locate ore veins using Visual Prospecting and begin mining Ilmenite and other Moon-exclusive ores.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
Space has no gravity in some areas, different atmospheric pressures on every body, and environmental hazards that differ from anything in the overworld. Arrive without full oxygen equipment and you will die quickly. Prepare thoroughly before launching — a failed expedition wastes all your rocket materials.
</Warning>

## The Moon

The Moon is the first destination and provides access to critical materials needed for IV-tier progression.

**Key ores:**
- **Ilmenite** — the primary source of Titanium, which is required for IV machine casings
- **Olivine** — yields Magnesium and other minerals useful in processing chains
- **Cheese** — a novelty block; less useful for progression

The Moon has **no atmosphere**. You require oxygen at all times outside a sealed environment. Oxygen Sealers create breathable zones inside enclosed structures, but any gap breaks the seal. Plan your base layout carefully.

**Space Stations** can be constructed in lunar orbit for permanent outposts — useful for storing resources and equipment without making the surface trip repeatedly.

**Moon Dungeons** appear on the surface and contain loot chests with useful materials and occasionally rare items that speed up progression.

## Mars

Mars is the Tier 2 destination and provides ores unavailable on the Moon or in the overworld.

**Key ores:**
- **Tetrahedrite** — a copper-bearing ore with useful byproducts in GT processing chains
- **Magnesium** — found in new mineralogical forms distinct from overworld sources
- **Aluminium** — additional deposits supplement overworld supply

Mars has a **thin atmosphere** — not quite a vacuum, but pressure protection is still required. The environmental suit requirements are similar to the Moon, but Martian mobs are more aggressive and the terrain more difficult to navigate.

## Galaxy Space planets

Galaxy Space expands the solar system with bodies accessible via Tier 3 and higher rockets. These planets are IV+ content — do not attempt them without appropriate gear and power infrastructure.

| Planet/Moon | Notable hazards | Key resources |
|-------------|----------------|---------------|
| Venus | Extreme heat, crushing pressure | Unique ore deposits |
| Io (Jupiter moon) | Volcanic, extreme radiation | High-tier ores |
| Europa (Jupiter moon) | Ice, ocean beneath | Rare minerals |
| Titan (Saturn moon) | Methane atmosphere | Unique chemistry |
| Oberon (Uranus moon) | Cold, low gravity | Exclusive ore veins |
| Triton (Neptune moon) | Extreme cold | Late-game materials |
| Pluto | Near-absolute-zero | ZPM-tier precursors |
| Ceres | Asteroid belt, low gravity | Concentrated ore deposits |

Each planet has **unique atmospheric challenges**: some require heat protection, others pressure suits, others radiation shielding. Arriving unprepared on a Galaxy Space planet is more immediately lethal than the Moon or Mars.

Progress through Galaxy Space planets is required for **ZPM and UV tier materials**. The processing chains for these materials exist on specific planets and cannot be replicated in the overworld.

## Amun-Ra

Amun-Ra is an additional dimension added by the amunra mod, providing unique resources not found elsewhere in the solar system. It is accessible in the late game and contains exclusive ore deposits tied to high-tier crafting chains.

<Tip>
Read the Galacticraft quest tab in the questbook before launching your first rocket — it provides step-by-step guidance for space progression, including which ores to prioritise, how to set up oxygen infrastructure, and what equipment to bring for each destination.
</Tip>
