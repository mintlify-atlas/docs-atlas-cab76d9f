GT New Horizons uses GregTech's voltage tiers as the backbone of all progression. Rather than unlocking content through exploration or boss kills alone, GTNH gates technology behind the steady accumulation of materials, machines, and knowledge. Each tier demands the previous tier's components to craft anything in the next — you cannot skip rungs on this ladder. The result is a modpack where progress feels genuinely earned, and where the transition from crude bronze pipes to humming electrical networks to orbital rockets is a coherent story of technological advancement.

## The tier system

GTNH's progression moves through three broad eras: a pre-electric age relying on manual labour and steam, an electric age spanning many voltage tiers, and a late-game space age that unlocks resources unavailable anywhere on the overworld.

### Stone age

Before any machines exist, you survive with stone and bronze tools crafted using GregTech's modified recipes. Vanilla wood and stone tools are replaced or nerfed; GregTech expects you to work through flint, then stone, then bronze before electricity becomes a concept. This phase is short but unforgiving.

### Steam age

The first real technological milestone is the Coke Oven, followed by steam boilers and steam-powered machines. Steam machines run on steam rather than EU, making them the bridge between manual crafting and electricity. Steel production — gated behind the Primitive Blast Furnace — marks the end of the Steam Age and the beginning of the electric era.

### LV — Low Voltage (8 EU/t)

The first tier of electricity introduces basic machines: Macerators, Chemical Reactors, Centrifuges, and Electrolyzers. Circuit crafting begins here. Applied Energistics 2 becomes accessible, and ore doubling via electric Macerators replaces steam-based methods. Coal Generators and Wind Mills provide early power.

### MV — Medium Voltage (32 EU/t)

MV unlocks the Electric Blast Furnace (EBF), which is required for smelting aluminium, steel in quantity, and other mid-game alloys. More processing machines come online: the Chemical Bath, Thermal Centrifuge, and Arc Furnace. Solar panels become viable for supplemental generation. Forestry bees begin contributing to resource production.

### HV — High Voltage (128 EU/t)

Large multi-block machines become viable at HV: the Large Chemical Reactor, Distillation Tower, and advanced circuit production. AE2 networks can scale significantly. The first Galacticraft rocket components become craftable, setting the stage for space exploration.

### EV — Extreme Voltage (512 EU/t)

EV represents the middle game. Industrial-scale processing lines come together, and space exploration becomes genuinely viable. Endgame mod content begins unlocking. Most players spend considerable time here before pushing into IV.

### IV — Insane Voltage (2048 EU/t)

IV is the gateway to the late game. It requires space-exclusive materials from the Moon and Mars, which is why space is not optional — it is a prerequisite. Fusion Reactor assembly begins at this tier. Circuit complexity reaches a new level of depth.

### LuV — Ludicrous Voltage (8192 EU/t)

LuV demands even more exotic materials sourced from outer planets accessible only through Galacticraft tier 2 and 3 rockets. Assembly Lines become essential for crafting components that cannot be made in a crafting table.

### ZPM — Zero Point Module (32768 EU/t)

ZPM-tier machines process at speeds and scales that dwarf earlier tiers. The materials needed at this stage require dedicated processing lines on remote planets. Most players who reach ZPM have hundreds of hours invested in the pack.

### UV — Ultimate Voltage (131072 EU/t)

UV is near-endgame. Machines at this tier approach the limits of what the pack's power infrastructure can deliver. Automation must be thoroughly designed to keep up with throughput demands.

### UHV and beyond

UHV, UEV, UIV, and higher tiers represent the extreme endgame, reached by only the most dedicated players. These tiers push against the theoretical limits of GregTech's energy system and require materials and processing chains of extraordinary complexity.

## Tier reference table

| Tier | EU/t | Key unlock | Approximate time to reach |
|------|------|-----------|--------------------------|
| Stone Age | — | Flint and bronze tools | First day |
| Steam Age | — | Coke Oven, steam machines, Steel | Days 1–7 |
| LV | 8 | Electric machines, AE2, basic circuits | Week 1–2 |
| MV | 32 | Electric Blast Furnace, aluminium | Weeks 2–4 |
| HV | 128 | Large multiblocks, Distillation Tower | Weeks 4–8 |
| EV | 512 | Space access, industrial processing | Months 1–3 |
| IV | 2048 | Fusion Reactor, space-gated materials | Months 3–6 |
| LuV | 8192 | Assembly Lines, outer planet ores | Months 6–9 |
| ZPM | 32768 | ZPM-tier processing | Months 9–12+ |
| UV | 131072 | Near-endgame machines | 12+ months |
| UHV+ | 524288+ | Extreme endgame | Veteran players only |

<Note>
Most players spend weeks or months in each major tier group — this is not a speedrun. The pack is designed to be a long-term project, and rushing through tiers by skipping steps is not possible: each tier requires the previous tier's materials to craft its components.
</Note>
