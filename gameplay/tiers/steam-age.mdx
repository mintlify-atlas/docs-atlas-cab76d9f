The Steam Age is the first major technological phase in GT New Horizons, beginning the moment you spawn and ending when you complete your first piece of Steel. Everything in this period runs without electricity — power comes from burning fuel in boilers, and machines are driven by pressurised steam through bronze pipes. It is a deliberately slow phase that teaches resource management and multiblock construction before the complexity of electric tiers arrives. Do not underestimate it: a well-built Steam Age foundation makes every subsequent tier easier.

## Pre-steam: stone and bronze tools

GregTech replaces most vanilla tool recipes. You will not be punching wood into planks and crafting a workbench in thirty seconds.

Your first tools are made from **flint**, harvested by breaking gravel or crafting flint pieces together. Flint tools are fragile but functional for the first night. From there, the goal is bronze.

**Bronze** is an alloy of copper and tin in a 3:1 ratio. Smelt both ores in a furnace, then combine the resulting dusts (or ingots, depending on your early recipe access) to produce bronze dust, which smelts into bronze ingots. Bronze tools are durable enough to carry you through the Steam Age.

**Wooden and bronze pipes** are used for early fluid handling. Bronze fluid pipes are your first way to move water and steam between structures — they appear basic but are essential infrastructure from the start.

## Coke Oven — your first multiblock

The Coke Oven is a 3×3×3 multiblock structure made entirely of **Coke Oven Bricks**, which are crafted from clay and sand. It has no controller block in the traditional sense — instead, right-clicking the assembled structure opens its interface.

The Coke Oven performs two functions simultaneously:

- Converts **Coal → Coal Coke**, a higher-energy fuel required for Steel production
- Produces **Creosote Oil** as a byproduct, used for wood treatment and as a material in later recipes

Coal Coke burns longer than regular coal and is the required fuel input for the Primitive Blast Furnace. Do not skip building this structure — without it, Steel is unreachable.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Craft Coke Oven Bricks">
    Combine 2 Clay Balls, 2 Sand, and 1 Gravel in a crafting grid to produce Coke Oven Bricks. You need enough to fill a 3×3×3 hollow cube — 26 bricks in total.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Build the 3×3×3 shell">
    Place Coke Oven Bricks in a 3×3 square on the ground, then build up two more layers to create a hollow cube with a 1×1×1 interior space. The structure must have no gaps or mismatched blocks.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify the structure">
    Right-click any Coke Oven Brick in the structure. If assembled correctly, the interface opens and the bricks display a connected texture. If the interface does not open, check for missing blocks or incorrect placement.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Load coal and collect output">
    Insert Coal into the input slot. The oven will slowly convert it to Coal Coke and fill the Creosote Oil tank. Collect both outputs before they overflow.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Steam Boilers

Steam is the energy carrier of this age. Boilers burn fuel to produce steam, which is piped to steam machines through bronze or steel fluid pipes.

- **Bronze Small Steam Boiler**: the first boiler you can build. Burns coal or wood, produces a modest amount of steam per tick. Takes time to heat up before producing steam at full rate.
- **Steel Large Steam Boiler**: higher steam output. Requires Steel, so it comes after the Blast Furnace. Worth building once Steel is available.

Boilers require a heat-up period after placing fuel — they do not produce steam instantly. Plan accordingly when starting a new session.

## Steam machines

All steam machines consume steam rather than EU. They accept steam input through their side ports and require bronze fluid pipes to connect to a boiler.

| Machine | Function |
|---------|----------|
| Steam Macerator | Grinds ores into dust for ore doubling |
| Steam Extractor | Extracts rubber from resin and other materials |
| Steam Compressor | Compresses items (required for some recipes) |
| Steam Furnace | Smelts items faster than a vanilla furnace |

<Tip>
Build multiple Steam Macerators — ore doubling is critical for efficient resource use. A single Macerator becomes a bottleneck quickly once you start processing ore veins in volume.
</Tip>

<Warning>
Steam machines require steam pipes to receive power — regular item or fluid pipes from other mods will not work. Use bronze fluid pipes, and ensure they connect to the correct face of the machine.
</Warning>

## Primitive Blast Furnace — the path to Steel

The **Primitive Blast Furnace** (sometimes called the Bronze Blast Furnace or simply Blast Furnace) is a 3×3×4 multiblock structure made from Blast Furnace Bricks. It is the second major multiblock of the Steam Age and the only way to produce Steel.

Steel requires:

- **Iron ingots or iron dust** as the base material
- **Coal Coke** (from the Coke Oven) as fuel

The Blast Furnace is slow. A single steel ingot takes several minutes to produce. Build it early and keep it running continuously.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Craft Blast Furnace Bricks">
    Blast Furnace Bricks are made from Nether Brick and Magma Cream in a crafting grid. Collect enough to assemble the 3×3×4 structure — you need bricks for the outer shell, leaving a 1×3×2 interior cavity.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assemble the 3×3×4 shell">
    Build the outer structure layer by layer. The Blast Furnace is four blocks tall and three blocks wide on each side. Leave the interior hollow — the furnace chamber forms inside.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Locate and interact with the controller">
    The Primitive Blast Furnace does not use a separate controller block — right-click any brick in the completed structure to open the interface and confirm it is properly assembled.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Load inputs and run">
    Insert Iron ingots (or dust) into the input slot and Coal Coke into the fuel slot. The furnace will slowly produce Steel ingots. Keep the Coal Coke supply stocked — it is consumed continuously.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Completing the Steam Age

The Steam Age ends when you have produced enough Steel to begin crafting LV (Low Voltage) machine casings and components. Steel is the foundation material for all electric machines — without it, you cannot progress to electricity. Before moving on, ensure you have:

- A steady Coke Oven producing Coal Coke
- At least one Primitive Blast Furnace running
- A bank of Steam Macerators doubling your ore output
- Sufficient bronze fluid pipe infrastructure connecting your boilers to your machines

With Steel in hand, the Electric Age begins.
